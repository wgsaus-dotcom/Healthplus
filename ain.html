WHO THIS IS FOR

Are you looking for purposeful AIN work?

Regional and remote NSW communities have a genuine, immediate need for skilled AINs. Whether you are based in Australia or trained overseas, we have a pathway for you.

Australian-based AINs
  • Cert III or IV in Individual Support or Nursing
  • Looking for a new region or a different challenge
  • Wanting access to the best placements in the sector
  • Seeking career growth and professional development
  • Open to temporary, contract or permanent work
Overseas-trained care workers
  • Trained in the Philippines, India, UK or Ireland
  • Working through skills assessment and registration
  • Wanting support from application to first shift
  • Seeking a regional pathway with genuine community purpose
  • Looking for relocation and settlement support

The demand is real. Regional and remote NSW communities — including Far West, Western NSW, and Murrumbidgee Local Health Districts — are experiencing critical shortages of qualified care workers. AINs are the clinical backbone of community and residential care in these areas. If you hold a Cert III in Individual Support or equivalent, your skills are in immediate demand.

WHAT WE DO FOR YOU

Your career success is our commitment

We do not just match you to a job. We analyse your experience, understand your goals, and find the placement where your skills are the right fit — then we support you through every step.

Step 01
We analyse your profile in depth
We take the time to understand your qualifications, skills, work history, and career goals — not just the role you want to fill. We identify what makes you the right match for a specific community and facility.
Step 02
We find the right placement
We identify and recommend placements that accurately reflect your skills and expertise. We use current market knowledge across all six regional LHDs to match you to positions where you will thrive — and be genuinely valued.
Step 03
We prepare and place you
We coordinate the interview process, provide pre-placement briefings, and ensure all credentialling is complete before your first shift. We handle employment contracts, payroll, superannuation, and workers compensation. You arrive ready to work.
Step 04
We support your relocation
Moving to a regional or remote community is a significant step. We provide practical relocation guidance — accommodation referrals, community orientation, and on-the-ground support — so your transition is as smooth as possible.
Step 05
We stay with you through placement
Our commitment does not end on your first day. We check in regularly during probationary periods, address any concerns quickly, and ensure your employer obligations are being met. We are always reachable within 24 hours.
Step 06
We build your career, not just a placement
We maintain our relationship with you throughout your career. We update you on professional development opportunities, market demand, and career advancement pathways — so you are always moving forward, not just placed and forgotten.
ROLES WE PLACE

AIN placements across all care settings

We place Assistants in Nursing across residential, community and acute settings throughout regional and remote NSW. All placements are temporary, contract or permanent.

Residential Care
Residential Aged Care AIN
Supporting residents in aged care facilities with personal care, daily living assistance, and dignity-centred care. Working alongside enrolled nurses and care teams in high-need regional settings including Far West and Western NSW LHDs.
Cert III Individual Support (Ageing) or equivalent · Police checked · NDIS Worker Screening (where applicable)
Community Care
Community AIN — Home & NDIS
Delivering personal care, mobility support, and community participation assistance to clients in their homes. Supporting NDIS participants and older Australians in rural and remote communities where access to care is most limited.
Cert III Individual Support or Home & Community Care · NDIS Worker Screening Check
Acute Support
AIN — Hospital & Clinical Settings
Providing patient care support in regional hospitals and multi-purpose services across Hunter New England, Northern NSW, and Mid North Coast LHDs. Assisting nursing staff with patient hygiene, mobility, observation, and comfort care.
Cert III or IV in Nursing Assistance · Current police clearance · AHPRA-compliant referencing
Rehab & Allied
AIN — Rehabilitation & Therapy Support
Supporting allied health teams in regional rehabilitation centres and outpatient services. Assisting physiotherapists, occupational therapists and other clinicians in implementing therapy programs with patients in remote communities.
Cert III Individual Support or equivalent · Experience in rehab settings preferred · Police checked

YOUR PATHWAY — FROM OVERSEAS TO REGIONAL NSW
Stage 01
Skills & qualification assessment
We review your overseas qualifications, identify any bridging requirements, and connect you with the right assessment body for your role. For aged care workers, this includes recognition of prior learning and Australian workplace standards.
Stage 02
Registration & compliance
We guide you through all Australian registration requirements — from National Police Clearances to NDIS Worker Screening Checks and reference verification. We work closely with you from the preliminary stages through to full compliance. For more on Australian visa and registration requirements, visit www.diac.gov.au.
Stage 03
Employability & soft skills
We provide industry-specific training referrals to develop your Australian workplace employability, sector-specific practices, and the soft skills that help you integrate confidently into a regional care team from day one.
Stage 04
Placement, relocation & settlement
Once credentialled, we coordinate your placement in a regional or remote NSW community. We assist with accommodation, community orientation, and practical relocation support — and we stay in contact throughout your placement to ensure everything is running well.
