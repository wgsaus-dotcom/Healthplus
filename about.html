Our Story

Built from the ground up
for regional Australia.

HealthPlus International was founded in 2026 with a clear-eyed understanding of a problem that had existed for decades — regional and remote NSW communities were chronically under-resourced with healthcare workers, and the organisations designed to solve it weren't built for it.

Most workforce providers operate from cities, for cities. Regional placements are treated as difficult, expensive afterthoughts. Communities in Broken Hill, Walgett, Bourke, and Lightning Ridge were left to navigate a system that wasn't designed with them in mind.

We built HealthPlus International specifically to serve these communities. Not as a side offering. Not as a niche add-on. As our entire purpose.

We are a labour hire employer of record — we employ every healthcare worker we place, carry all employment obligations, and give our clients a single all-inclusive invoice. No HR complexity. No compliance risk. Just the right person, in the right community.

The workforce exists. The communities are there. What's missing is the connection — and the infrastructure to make it work reliably and compliantly at scale. That's exactly what HealthPlus International provides.

Our Motto

Three words.
Everything we stand for.

P
01

People.

The word comes first because it is what this entire business is built around. Not transactions. Not placements. People.

The healthcare workers who leave their families to serve distant communities deserve to be treated as professionals — fair pay, proper support, clear expectations, and an employer who has their back.

The community members in Broken Hill, Walgett, Bourke, and Lightning Ridge who depend on care workers for daily support deserve the same quality of care as anyone in a major city. Full stop.

Every decision we make starts with people — not metrics, not margins.

C
02

Care.

Care is both what we deliver and how we operate.

What we deliver is direct — we place healthcare workers in communities carrying disproportionate health burdens. High rates of chronic disease, ageing populations, disability needs, and mental health challenges that go unmet not because the community doesn't deserve support, but because the workforce hasn't been there.

How we operate reflects the same value. We brief every worker before they arrive. We support them through the engagement. We respond within 24 hours because when a community is short-staffed, time matters.

Care isn't a value we display on a wall. It is the standard we hold every placement to.

C
03

Compliance.

In healthcare and community services, compliance is not a checkbox. It is the foundation of trust.

For the facilities we work with — every worker is credentialled, police checked, and has confirmed working rights before they set foot in your organisation. You receive a full compliance summary. You take on zero employment obligation. One invoice.

For the workers we employ — Fair Work compliance, correct Award rates, superannuation, and workers' compensation are managed by us. They focus on the work. We handle the rest.

Compliance runs through everything we do. It protects the communities, the workers, and the organisations we serve.

Our Leadership

The person behind HealthPlus.

Abhay J Kumar founded HealthPlus International with a specific conviction — that the healthcare workforce challenge in regional and remote NSW was a solvable problem, and that solving it required an organisation built specifically for it, not one trying to serve metro and regional markets simultaneously.

With over 20 years of experience in the labour hire industry, Abhay brings a depth of knowledge in workforce deployment, employer of record models, compliance frameworks, and the operational realities of placing people in complex and demanding environments. He understands what it takes to get the right person to the right place — and to keep the employment relationship working properly throughout.

More directly relevant to HealthPlus International's mission is his 5 years of hands-on experience as a support worker provider — working at the interface between community need and workforce capacity. That experience is not theoretical. It is the lived understanding of what facilities require, what workers need, and what communities deserve — built through years of actually doing it, not managing it from a distance.

His approach is grounded in three principles that map directly to the company's motto: put people first in every decision, deliver care to communities that have been underserved for too long, and operate with a standard of compliance that gives both clients and workers complete confidence.
