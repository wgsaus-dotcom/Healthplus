The remote allied health crisis

The allied health workforce is there.
Regional communities are ready. We connect them.

There are credentialled allied health professionals across Australia who are motivated to work in regional and remote communities. And there are communities with real, unmet health needs. The gap between them is a connection problem — and bridging it is what we do.

CRITICAL

Mental Health — Far West

Psychologist-to-population ratio in Far West NSW is among the lowest in Australia. Wait times exceed 12 months in many communities.

CRITICAL

Podiatry — Western NSW

Diabetes rates in remote Aboriginal communities are 3× higher than metro. Podiatry access is essential to prevent avoidable amputations.

HIGH

Physiotherapy — Murrumbidgee

Farming and physical labour injuries are common, yet physio access is severely limited. Many residents travel 3+ hours for treatment.

HIGH

OT — Hunter New England

Aged care residents and community members in rural HNE LHD face long delays for OT assessments essential to home modifications and equipment funding.

HIGH

Speech Pathology — Remote

Childhood language delays in Aboriginal communities often go undetected without speech pathology services. Long-term educational impacts.

COMMON QUESTIONS

Allied Health Placement — FAQs

Questions about how we place allied health professionals in regional NSW.

We place physiotherapists, occupational therapists, psychologists, podiatrists, speech pathologists, dietitians, exercise physiologists, Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander health practitioners, medical radiation practitioners, dental professionals, and optometrists. We focus on professions with the highest shortage burden in regional and remote NSW.
Yes. All allied health professionals placed by HealthPlus International must hold current AHPRA registration in their relevant profession. We verify registration status before every deployment. Workers whose registration is lapsed, suspended, or conditional are not deployed.
Yes — this is our core focus. We place allied health professionals specifically in regional and remote NSW communities including Broken Hill, Walgett, Bourke, Griffith, Moree, Tamworth, and surrounding areas. We do not operate in metropolitan Sydney.
HealthPlus International is the employer of record for all workers we place. Allied health professionals are employed directly by us — we handle payroll, superannuation, workers compensation, and compliance. The facility receives a single all-inclusive invoice and manages clinical work on site.
We respond to all requests within 24 hours. Placement timelines for allied health vary by profession and region — physiotherapists and OTs typically have the fastest turnaround. We maintain an active candidate network across all allied health disciplines we place.
